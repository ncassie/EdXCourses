The White Mountains cover about a quarter of the state of New Hampshire and a small portion of western Maine in the United States.

They are part of the northern Appalachian Mountains and the considered most rugged mountains in New England.

Most of the White Mountians are public land, and are located in the White Mountain National Forest and a number of state parks.
